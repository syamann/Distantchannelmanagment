Distant Channel Management

A Software That Helps Field Teams To Organize And Digitize Customer Meetings.

What Is DCM?

Modern Innovative

A DCM, short of a ‘distant channel management’ is a software that helps field teams to organize and digitize customer meetings.

DCM software can be used for tracking all the customer meetings that have been made via video conferencing system.

Applying customer visits in a physical environment is a long-gone history. With the pandemic and the digital transformation trend coming together, these visits tend to turn into video conferences.

DCM helps you to move your field efforts to the digital landscape by using a video conferencing application. You can apply your meetings via DCM software, while planning and reporting processes happen in the same place.

Digital transformation of the field teams is a different process than other departments. Using a video conference platform that is not specifically designed for field teams would fall short of meeting their needs.

While working remotely, field teams need to:

Take Orders

Fill The Questionnaires

Apply The Meetings

Report Their Efforts

With a ‘distant channel management’ software, field teams can reach all these features
via one platform. It is an all-around solution for all the digital needs of the field teams.

Why DCM?

Why Does Your Company Need To Use Distant Channel Management?

With distant channel management, your company’s field team would optimize their daily efforts. However, DCM offers a large variety of features that makes this software viable not only for field teams, but also for different parts of the company such as HR, or IT.

DCM is generally best for companies that have a bigger portion of use for their field teams, such as pharmaceutical companies, or cosmetic companies. Field agents of such companies are known for their efforts to meet more customers throughout the day. A DCM software would be beneficial for them to make the best out of their efforts.

These are not the only examples, food companies, clothing companies, or any company that includes mass distribution in their organization would benefit from the advantages of the DCM such as being able to meet more customers via video conferencing.

B2B service providers, SaaS companies would benefit from the DCM, too. First order for those companies would be crucial to continue to get funds. Optimizing customer meetings would help them to achieve those goals in the long term.

When To Adopt DCM?

When To Adopt DCM To Your Company?

With The Information Above, You Wanted To Adopt DCM Software To Your Company’s Organization. Now, You’re Asking Yourself At Which Stage Should I Do It?

DCM can benefit a large variety of institutions, from an emerging start-up to an international company that is working in several countries.

In the starting stage, businesses tend to keep track of their customer visits via several programs. Calendar applications may be useful for the planning process, and Microsoft Excel or Google Sheets for keeping the reports. And yes, there are lots of video conferencing applications to conduct these meetings, of course, if it was decided to be online.

As your business grows, it would get harder to keep track of them together. You would need integration between your calendar and video conference application. Then, agents would need the customer information, reports from previous meetings, or the preferences of the customer during the meeting. Opening another application while conducting a meeting would make things messier. As your business grows, it will get more and more complicated.

DCM is offering you a grand view for tracking all these. With DCM, you can reach the necessary information about the customer on the meeting screen. You can take orders, apply questionnaires, and see the notes of a customer while making a remote visit without getting in the mess of different softwares.

After the interview, you can fill the report about it from DCM software, and this information will be stored in the cloud to help you in your next meeting with the customer

It will help with the HR Issues, too. If one of your agents leaves, precious information about the customer would not vanish with him. All information is stored in the cloud, your next hired agent can take from where the previous agent left off.

How To DCM Helps?

DCM is designed to make life easier for the field departments of the companies. Being value-driven, makes it possible. But, what are those values that make it viable and profitable for them?

Time Management

DCM helps you to save time. First of all, conducting field visits physically needs transportation. Which takes a lot of time, especially if your company is operating in metropolitans.

Most field teams had thought about making visits online, but the negatives about this approach were dominant. But DCM makes this trade-off positive, with the easy tracking, built-in applying, and detailed reporting via one platform.

With DCM, you may easily move your field efforts to the digital, and save the time that your field team spent on the roads.

Being able to track all the processes from one platform, would save you from the confusion of using tens of different programs to track just a meeting. Thus, DCM would give your time back by giving a clearer view of the processes of your field teams.

Cost Reduction

DCM helps you to save money. The cost of maintaining a field team that is making physical meetings is super high. If you do not want to look amateur by forcing your field agents to travel with public buses, you need to hire a car fleet.

As your business grows, this car fleet will expand. That will increase maintenance costs, fuel costs, etc. As your company grows, your expenses will grow, too.

By transforming your field team to digital with the help of DCM, you will get rid of these expenses! DCM is a value-driven software. It helps you to save two of the most important values that a man has, money and time.
